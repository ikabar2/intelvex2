YOU COULD ALREADY BE
OUT OF COMPLIANCE.

New York's SHIELD Act and Illinois' Biometric Information Privacy Act (BIPA) carry real fines — and they apply to small businesses. Most owners don't know they're exposed. We'll tell you exactly where you stand and how to fix it.

NY SHIELD Act

Requires reasonable data security for any NY resident's data. No size exemption. Penalties up to $250K.

Illinois BIPA

Fines of $1,000–$5,000 per biometric data violation. Applies to timeclocks, facial recognition, and more.

HIPAA (Healthcare)

Mandatory for NY/IL clinics, practices, and any business that handles patient health information.

ABA Rule 1.6

Illinois and New York attorneys must make reasonable efforts to prevent client data breaches or face ethics violations.
